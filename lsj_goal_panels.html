Watershed Goals

The Lower San Juan Nine Element Plan identifies four water quality goals. Each goal links directly to the interactive map and spatial data explorer for that topic.

Goal 1 — Sediment & Salinity

Reduce Erosion and Salinity Loading

Excess sediment and salt enter the Lower San Juan from erosion-prone landscapes, irrigated fields, and naturally saline formations like Mancos Shale. This goal targets priority source areas to improve water clarity and protect aquatic life and agriculture.

Where to look on the map
McElmo Creek Chinle Creek Lower Montezuma Creek Springs (Lay 2025)
What's being done
Erosion control — check dams, rock structures, contour farming, cover crops
Salinity reduction — irrigation efficiency, tailwater recovery, canal lining
Rangeland management — brush control, reseeding, grazing plans
Riparian restoration — grassed waterways, sediment basins, native replanting
Water quality monitoring at priority sediment and salinity source areas

Goal 2 — Bacteria

Improve Conditions for Recreation and Cultural Uses

Elevated bacteria levels — primarily from livestock and failing septic systems — limit safe swimming, fishing, and cultural practices along key reaches. This goal supports public health and safe use of the San Juan River and its tributaries through targeted livestock and wastewater management.

Where to look on the map
San Juan River — Bluff to Mexican Hat Lower Montezuma Creek Recapture Creek Montezuma tributaries
What's being done
Livestock management — riparian fencing, off-stream water tanks, rotational grazing
Waste management — manure handling practices and seasonal grazing exclusion
Stream buffers — riparian filter strips to reduce runoff reaching waterways
Septic system upgrades — maintenance programs, community leachfields, sewer projects
Public outreach and education on water quality and safe recreation

Goal 3 — Metals & Legacy Sites

Address Metals, Selenium & Radionuclides from Legacy Sources

Decades of uranium mining, oil field operations, and naturally occurring selenium create localized but serious water quality risks in the Lower San Juan. This goal focuses on reducing pollutant loading to protect aquatic life, drinking water sources, and communities near legacy sites.

Where to look on the map
Lower Montezuma Creek Four Corners / Lower San Juan White Mesa Mill area Aneth & Montezuma Valley
What's being done
Passive treatment — constructed wetlands and bioreactors to filter contaminated water
Mine reclamation — stabilizing tailings, regrading, revegetating, installing diversion channels
Irrigation management — converting flood to drip irrigation, tailwater retention basins
Soil amendments — gypsum and organic treatments to reduce selenium mobility
Multi-agency coordination — mine cleanup, monitoring, interagency partnerships

Goal 4 — Hydrology & Riparian

Restore Streamflow, Riparian Habitat & Watershed Resilience

Altered hydrology, invasive plants, and degraded riparian areas reduce the watershed's ability to store water, support native habitat, and buffer against drought and flood. This goal works across jurisdictions to restore baseflows, remove invasive species, and reconnect streams to their floodplains.

Where to look on the map
Bears Ears canyon systems Abajo Mountains headwaters San Juan River riparian corridors Private irrigated lands
What's being done
Stream restoration — beaver dam analogs and floodplain reconnection to slow and spread water
Spring and wetland protection — fencing, wet meadow rehab, livestock watering alternatives
Invasive species removal — tamarisk and Russian olive clearing along river corridors
Native replanting — revegetating cleared areas with native riparian species
Interagency partnerships — coordinated forest and fuels management in priority watersheds
